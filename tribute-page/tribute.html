Karel Čapek

Karel Čapek was a great Czech writer renowned for his science fiction works. The word Robot, invented by his brother Josef, was first used in Čapek's drama R.U.R. In his books and plays, Karel Čapek reflected contemporary society issues and dealt with inner life of human. He actively opposed fascism as well as communism.

Timeline of Čapek's life

  • 1890 - Born on the 9th of January in the village of Malé Svatoňovice
  • 1909 - Finishes the Academic Grammar School
  • 1915 - Graduates with a doctorate of philosophy at Charles University in Prague
  • 1917 - Starts working as an editor for the Czech paper Národní listy (The National Newspaper). In 2021 he leaves and joins the staff of Lidové noviny (The People's Paper)
  • 1925 - Becomes the first chairman of Czechoslovak PEN club and holds office until 1933. In 1925 he also starts holding regular Friday meetings with cultural and political important figures, including President T.G. Masaryk
  • 1935 - Marries actress Olga Scheinpflugová, after a long acquaintance
  • 1938 - Dies of pneumonia on the 25th of December at the age of 48
